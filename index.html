Chase the Light

Privacy Policy

Overview

Chase the Light is a photography light-phase tracker built for iOS. This policy describes what information the app accesses, how it is used, and what is never collected.

The short version: your data stays on your device. Nothing you do in Chase the Light is collected, stored, or shared with anyone.

Location Data

Chase the Light uses your location only while the app is open. Location is used solely to calculate sun position, light phase timings, and to display your city name. It is never transmitted to any server, never stored in any database, and never shared with third parties.

Specifically, your location is used to:

  • Calculate the current sun altitude and azimuth for your position
  • Compute sunrise, sunset, and all six light phase windows for today
  • Display the name of your city or region on screen
  • Position an indicator dot on the live satellite image

All of these calculations happen entirely on your device using Apple's CoreLocation and standard solar geometry. Your coordinates are never sent anywhere.

Satellite Imagery

The app downloads live satellite images from NOAA's public GOES-East feed at cdn.star.nesdis.noaa.gov. This is an unauthenticated public endpoint. No identifying information about you or your device is sent as part of this request beyond what a standard HTTPS connection includes (your IP address, managed by Apple's networking stack).

Data We Do Not Collect

  • No account or sign-in required
  • No analytics or usage tracking
  • No advertising or ad identifiers
  • No crash reporting services
  • No location history or logs
  • No data shared with third parties

Children's Privacy

Chase the Light does not collect any personal information from anyone, including children under 13. The app complies with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA).
